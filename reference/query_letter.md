Dear [AGENT NAME],

I am seeking representation for my memoir, *THE IMPROBABILITY OF ME* (est. 75,000 words), a narrative nonfiction account of how I went from being labeled a federal "Kingpin" on the streets of Baltimore to becoming a Principal OT Cybersecurity Engineer at Walmart and a co-author of NIST 800-82—the federal government's definitive guide to securing America's critical infrastructure.

At fifteen, I stabbed a man with a folding knife on North Avenue in Baltimore after five grown men ambushed me at a bus stop. The locking mechanism failed mid-strike. I never carried a folding blade again. At twenty-five, I was working on Air Force Two at Andrews Air Force Base when federal agents kicked down my door and arrested me on fourteen felony counts for distributing anabolic steroids through an Eastern European supply chain that shipped product inside factory-sealed "Happy Birthday" cans. The government called me a Kingpin. I called it a side hustle gone wrong during an era when Mark McGwire was a national hero for using the same chemicals.

After four months in lockup and eighteen months on house arrest, I pulled up my pant leg during a job interview, showed the hiring manager my ankle monitor, and asked if it would be a problem. He said, "Everyone deserves a second chance. Start Monday." That was the beginning of a two-decade climb through Northrop Grumman, the nuclear industry, Koch Industries, and MITRE—the government think tank that solves problems for a safer world—before testicular cancer and a full hip replacement forced me to recalibrate what survival actually means.

*THE IMPROBABILITY OF ME* will appeal to readers of Tara Westover's *Educated*, David Goggins' *Can't Hurt Me*, and Nick Bilton's *American Kingpin*. It combines the raw, street-level survival narrative of a Baltimore childhood with the technical precision of an engineer who treats every life event—from knife fights to cancer diagnoses—as a system failure to be analyzed and corrected.

I am a Principal OT Cybersecurity Engineer at Walmart, a co-author of NIST Special Publication 800-82 Rev 3, a TÜV Certified Functional Safety Engineer, and a former MITRE Principal Engineer. I hold a Master of Science in Systems Engineering from the Johns Hopkins University Whiting School of Engineering. I have been featured in [PUBLICATIONS/SPEAKING ENGAGEMENTS IF ANY]. This is my first book.

Thank you for your time and consideration. The full manuscript is available upon request.
