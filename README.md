# THE IMPROBABILITY OF ME

**A Memoir by Michael Thompson**

---

> *The Resume and The Rap Sheet. The Safety Mother and the Kingpin. The Engineer and the Survivor. This is the story of the man who lived all of those lives — simultaneously.*

## About This Book

This autobiography traces the arc of Michael Thompson's life through a series of dualities: the Baltimore streets and the nuclear control room, the felony record and the functional safety credential, the cancer ward and the C-Suite. It is the story of a man who should not exist, statistically speaking — and yet here he is.

## Structure

| I | **The Origins** | 1–5 | Baltimore childhood, Audrey Avenue, street survival |
| II | **The Service** | 6–8 | Air Force, Kadena/Okinawa, Thailand |
| III | **The Rise** | 9–10 | The Golden Boy, SAM FOX, the pivot to "the business" |

| VIII | **The Crash** | 22–23 | The C-Suite burnout, the final medical battle |
| — | **Epilogue** | — | The Improbability of Me |

## Writing Voice

- **"Accordion" expansion**: High-level technical/philosophical hooks → visceral, fast-paced action sequences
- **Gritty and sensory-focused** — the reader smells the bleach, hears the hiss
- **Engineer's lens** — analyzing life's "failure points" and "mechanisms" (both mechanical and social)
- **Self-reflective irony** — a man with a Rap Sheet mastering "Unescorted Access" and "Functional Safety"
- **First-person, present-tense action** — switches to reflective past-tense for analysis
