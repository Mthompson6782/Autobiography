# Chapter 21: The Purple Belt and the Gray Hair

> *STATUS: DRAFTED — from Gemini sessions.*

---

I was moving at the speed of light.

In Wichita, I wasn't just a Director; I was a machine. I was solving billion-dollar problems by day, and by night, I was on the mats at the **Wichita Jiu-Jitsu Club**.

I trained under **Chris Haines**. If you know the lineage, you know that rank under Haines isn't given; it's forged. I earned my **Purple Belt** there.

Purple is the rank where the game changes. You stop just collecting techniques and start understanding the *system* of leverage. You become dangerous in a quiet, technical way.

But I was redlining. Between the corporate pressure of the OpEx role, the MIT coursework, and the grind of the mats, I was ignoring the signals my body was sending me. I was too busy to realize my health was deteriorating. I just pushed through. That’s what wrestlers do. That’s what paratroopers do.

I set my sights on the next rung of the ladder: **President**.

There was an opening for the President role at **Koch Specialty Plant Services (KSPS)**. It was a perfect fit for my background in reliability, safety, and operations.

I interviewed. I laid out the strategy. I showed them the track record of resilience and the billions in protected assets.

They rejected me.

It wasn't because I lacked the skills. It wasn't because I lacked the vision.

*"Michael,"* they told me, *"you don't have enough gray hair to do this role."*

They meant it literally.

I looked at them. I was a Director. I was a Purple Belt. I was a TÜV Engineer. But I wasn't *old*. In their eyes, gravitas looked like wrinkles and silver hair, not dynamic energy.

I realized then that I had hit the **Ceiling of Optics**. I could be the guy who *did* the work, but I couldn't be the guy who *led* the company—not yet.

I didn't have to wait long for a rebuttal from the universe.

The CEO of a massive **Siemens Distributor**—a major player in the industrial automation space—called me. He didn't care about the color of my hair. He cared about the firepower in my brain.

*"I want you to be the Chief Engineer and Technology Officer,"* he said.

**CETO.** A C-Level executive role.

It was the vindication I needed. I packed up my life in Kansas. I said goodbye to the mats at Wichita Jiu-Jitsu.

I was heading back to **Houston**.
