# Chapter 20: The 1.7 Billion Dollar Jump and the Wichita HQ

> *STATUS: DRAFTED — from Gemini sessions.*

---

Leaving Dr. Angela Summers wasn't just a resignation; it was a divorce.

**Koch Industries** came calling. They wanted me to oversee a massive modernization effort for their fertilizer plants under **Koch Ag & Energy**. The scope was staggering: a **$1.7 billion** capital project.

I couldn't say no.

I walked into Angela’s office to break the news. She was pissed. Big time pissed. But I knew the game. Business isn't personal; it's arithmetic.

*"I know you're angry,"* I told her. *"But I'm going to be in charge of a 1.7 billion dollar budget. And we are going to need the best Functional Safety consultants in the world."*

I handed her a massive contract. I gave her firm a huge amount of work on the very project I was leaving to lead. She stopped screaming. Money heals all wounds.

I packed my bags for **Wichita, Kansas**.

I started as the **OpEx Automation Group Leader**.

My job was **Operational Excellence**. I wasn't just upgrading computers; I was responsible for using automation to drive efficiency, increase margins, and ensure these massive plants ran at peak performance.

The pressure was immense. If a fertilizer plant goes wrong, it’s a crater. We had to upgrade these systems while they were running, under budget, and safe.

I nailed it.

We brought the plants into the modern era. The leadership noticed. They didn't just give me a bonus; they gave me a new title.

**Director.**

I was promoted to **Director of Industrial Control System (ICS) Resilience**.

It was a hybrid role that only I could fill:

1. **Cybersecurity** (Nuclear background).
2. **Functional Safety** (Dr. Summers background).
3. **Operations/OpEx** (FMC and Koch background).

They also tossed me a "side job": **Disaster Recovery and Business Continuity (BCDR)**. I went to the **MIT Sloan School of Management** to get the frameworks.

I was solving problems that moved the needle by billions of dollars. I had reached the mountaintop.
