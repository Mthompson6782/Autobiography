# Chapter 9: The Happy Birthday Cans

> *STATUS: DRAFTED — pending review.*

---

The government loves a good origin story. When the feds finally built my profile, they painted me as a master strategist who sat down and meticulously blueprinted an international drug syndicate from scratch. 

The truth is much more mundane, and much more dangerous. I didn't set out to be a kingpin. I just wanted a discount. 

After I reached out to Spartak, my only goal was personal use. I was lifting heavy, spending hours in the gym, and packing on serious mass. The feeling of the steroids hitting your system is hard to describe if you haven't experienced it. It’s an incredible, intoxicating surge of sheer physical strength. You feel like the chassis has finally caught up with the engine. 

I was buying my cycles directly from Spartak in Eastern Europe, and the product was flawless. 

One day, Spartak reached out with a proposition. 

He had another client in the United States, but shipping directly to them was getting complicated. He asked if he could route that person's order to my address. If I agreed to take delivery of the package and simply re-mail it domestically to his client, Spartak would cut the price of my personal order in half. 

I looked at the math. Half off pharmaceutical-grade testosterone just for putting a box in the mail. It was a friction-less transaction. 

"Sure," I said. 

That single word was the spark that ignited the whole powder keg. 

It started with just that one extra package. I received it, dropped it in the mail, and got my discount. But Spartak was a businessman, and he recognized that he had just secured a reliable, disciplined domestic hub in the United States. I wasn't some sketchy junkie; I was a guy with military discipline, a house, and an understanding of logistics. 

Slowly, the requests increased. Spartak asked me to forward another package. Then another. 

Before I knew it, the orders I was receiving weren't just for one or two people. They were getting massive. The volume of product moving through my address was staggering, and the delivery mechanisms Spartak used to get it past customs became increasingly intricate and bizarre. 

There were no shady duffel bags handed off in dark alleys. This was an international supply chain. The gear would arrive hidden inside seemingly innocent objects. I remember opening one shipment and finding actual, factory-sealed metal cans—the kind you would buy at a novelty store. They were brightly colored and literally printed with the words "Happy Birthday." 

You popped the seal on the Happy Birthday can, and inside were thousands of dollars worth of anabolic steroids. 

It was wild. It was almost comical. But the money wasn't a joke, and neither was the volume. 

Without ever formally applying for the job, I had become the primary distribution node for a massive Eastern European operation. I was the funnel through which the product flowed into the United States. I was routing packages to the East Coast, the West Coast, and eventually South America. 

I was no longer just a guy trying to get big in the gym. I was the Kingpin the government was looking for. And I was hiding in plain sight, entirely surrounded by Happy Birthday cans.
