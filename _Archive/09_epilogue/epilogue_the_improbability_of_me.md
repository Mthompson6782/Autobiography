# Epilogue: The Improbability of Me

> *STATUS: Outline only — awaiting draft*

---

## The Hook
Run the numbers. Calculate the probability. A Black kid from Baltimore's Audrey Avenue, raised by an abuser, armed with a pen at fourteen, convicted of a felony, diagnosed with cancer — becoming a Director at Koch Industries and the Mother of Functional Safety. The probability is zero. And yet, here I am.

## Key Beats
- The statistical improbability — run the actual numbers on outcomes for kids from his background
- The duality resolved (or not): the resume and the rap sheet are both real, both permanent
- The engineer's final analysis of his own life — mechanisms, failure modes, safety margins
- What "functional safety" means when applied to a human life
- The unanswered question: was it intelligence and guile? Or was it something else entirely?
- The last word: not inspiration, not triumph, but *fact*. The improbability is the point.

## Tone
No sentimentality. No victory lap. The engineer looks at the data, acknowledges the anomaly, and moves on. Because that's what survivors do.
