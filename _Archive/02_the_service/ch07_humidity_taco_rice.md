## The Hook
Kadena Air Base sits on Okinawa like an American city transplanted into the Pacific. The humidity wraps around you like a second skin. The taco rice is the unofficial food of every airman who ever served there.

## Key Beats
- Kadena Air Base, Okinawa — the base, the mission, the daily rhythm
- Cultural immersion — Japan through the lens of a Baltimore kid
- Taco rice — the absurd fusion food that becomes a symbol of the military's cultural collisions
- The humidity as a physical presence — oppressive, inescapable, character-building
- Professional development — the technical skills beginning to form
- The camaraderie and the isolation of being far from everything you knew

## Sensory Anchors
- Okinawan heat and humidity
- The sounds of the base vs. the sounds of the island
- Taco rice — the taste, the spot, the ritual
