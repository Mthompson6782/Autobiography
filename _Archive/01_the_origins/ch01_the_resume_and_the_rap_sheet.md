# Chapter 1: The Résumé and The Rap Sheet

> *STATUS: Outline only — awaiting draft*

---

## The Hook
Two documents. Same name. One opens doors in corporate boardrooms. The other should have sealed them shut forever. Both are true. Both are Michael Thompson.

## Key Beats
- Introduce the central paradox of the book
- The reader should understand from page one that this is not an inspirational bootstraps story — it's an improbability
- Set the tone: analytical, self-aware, darkly funny
- The resume: nuclear safety, functional safety, Koch Industries, Director-level
- The rap sheet: felony conviction, the legal system's narrative of "the mastermind"

## Sensory Anchors
- The feel of a laminated badge vs. a mugshot printout
- Corporate elevator vs. courthouse elevator

## Bridge to Next Chapter
From the present-day paradox, pull the reader backward: *"To understand how both of these documents can belong to the same man, you have to start where all mechanisms start — at the point of manufacture."*
