## The Hook
Every system has a single point of failure. In childhood, that point is the person who's supposed to keep you safe.

## Key Beats
- Deeper portrait of Pauline — not just a monster, but a mechanism of a failing system
- The broader context: why was Michael in her care?
- The other children — witnesses, co-survivors
- The moment(s) of realization that this was not normal
- How cruelty shapes the operating system of a child's brain

## Sensory Anchors
- Specific objects, rooms, routines associated with the abuse
- The contrast between the outside world (school, neighborhood) and Pauline's domain

## Bridge
From the darkness of Pauline's house to the light of the Paratrooper — someone who finally taught Michael that strength wasn't just about surviving hits, but about learning to deliver them with purpose.
